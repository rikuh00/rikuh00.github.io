Communication

Leadership, Teamwork, and Professionalism

Communication is integral to the work of engineers; this includes reading and writing reports, giving presentations, and liaising with teammates and/or clients (both orally and via written messages). I have developed strong communication skills over the past years from coursework, extra-curricular activities, and internships - I am able to communicate effectively via written medium like reports and emails in particular. However, I am developing skills to communicate my thoughts in a group setting, particularly when it comes to communicating my opinions in difficult situations involving negotiations or conflict resolution.

The entries below demonstrate how I have used my communication skills in the Design for the Environment course.

Client Communication

As the team leader, I am in charge of communicating with the client via email to set up client meetings and follow up with a meeting summary after each meeting. Specifically, my role includes the following tasks:

    • Creating and sending a meeting agenda
      Liaising with team members to organize talking points during the meeting
      Understanding the client's intention and taking notes during the meeting
      Promptly sending a meeting summary after each meeting

    • Throughout the term, the client has been satistified with the team's self-initiative to reach out for questions and sugest ideas to make a better recommendation to the client - I believe this is partly because of how the team as a whole has stayed organized during client meetings to deliver information in a concise manner. By sending summaries after the meeting, I also help both the client and my team to ensure that both parties are on the same page about the progress of the project.

    This experience allowed me to demonstrate and improve my communication skills, particularly in the context of hosting client meetings and communicating with clients. If I end up working in finance as per my career goal, I would be talking to multiple clients in a day to get new deals. As such, this aspect of the course directly ties into developing the skills necessary for my future career.

    External Events Director - UofT Women in Finance

    As the external events director for UofT Women in Finance (UTWF), my main responsibility is to organize and execute events that target female STEM students who are interested in a career in finance. In this role, I have demonstrated my communication skills by maintaining positive relationships with 3 different firms to host a total of 4 events throughout the school year. In order to bring successful events to our members, I had to balance the members' needs with the interests of these companies - I did this by clearly communicating our expectations upfront but also being open to diffrent suggestions that the firms would bring up.

    In addition, I demonstrated my leadership skills by working effectively with 5 other executive members in the club to coordinate event schedules and promotions. For example, I worked collaboratively with our promotions director to create a campaign with our target audience in mind; this resulted in 150% increase in the event turnout. As a senior member in the club, I also demonstrate my leadership by mentoring junior executive members to foster strong relationships with professionals in the financial industry.
