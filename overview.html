A little bit about me...

As a second-year Mechanical Engineering student at University of Toronto, I am constantly learning new engineering tools and project management methods in order to succeed in my future careers. I chose engineering tools and project management as my main focus area because I am more interested in engineering design as opposed to research; in order to produce successful designs, I must know how to work with various engineering tools and create effective project management plans. Particularly, I am planning on pursuing specializations in biomedical engineering and mechatronics in order to achieve my career aspiration of working in either finance or biomedical engineering.

I became interested in the finance industry after a 4-month internship at Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (CPPIB). By completing three projects (index analysis, machine learning, and data visualization), I learned how to select, combine, and adapt engineering tools to solve complex problems. In addition, I demonstrated my strength in project management by creating detailed project management plans and delivering high-quality documents on time. These skills, along with my solid foundation in economics, would allow me to succeed in any role within the industry.

While my interest in finance stems from professional experiences, my interest in biomedical industry comes from my passion for engineering designs. Through hackathon experience at MakeUofT and undergraduate design courses like Engineering Strategies and Practice II, I gained an understanding of the engineering design process as well as design tools like SolidWorks. Although I do not have professional experiences in biomedical engineering, my competencies in engineering tools and project management make this industry a good fit for me; I hope to further improve these competencies by working in a field where I can make a positive impact on patientsâ€™ lives through transformative technology.
