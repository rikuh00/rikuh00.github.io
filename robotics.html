Robotics for Education

University of Toronto, January - April 2019

Introduction

For a first-year engineering design project, our team of 6 designed a robotics education kit for Braincubator, a STEM education start-up in Toronto. As the project manager of the team, I developed a detailed project management plan to track team progress, delegate work to members, and ensure that high-quality works are submitted on time.

Project Overview

Braincubator is an educational start-up in Toronto that aims to teach engineering design thinking and robotics to students across Greater Toronto Area. We designed a robotics kit that allows students to assemble their own robots, using components from Arduino toolkit. Based on our conceptual design, we created a prototype using AutoCAD and Arduino to show how the robot is assembled by a student.

By swapping tubes that have different electronic components at the end (sensors, camera, LED light, etc.), students can customize their robots easily to create personal projects.

Tools Used

  • AutoCAD: prototyping the housing and tubes for the robot
  • Arduino: controlling electronic elements on the robot
  • Microsoft Project: tracked team progress and assigned work to colleagues
